About

I am a PhD candidate in the Department of Agricultural, Environmental, and Development Economics (AEDE) at The Ohio State University. My work lies at the intersection of industrial organization and applied microeconomics, with a focus on oligopoly theory, strategic firm behavior, and development economics.

I build game-theoretic models — and take them to data — to understand how firms compete, how bribery and corruption shape market entry in emerging economies, and how market structure responds to shocks such as natural disasters and antitrust enforcement. I currently serve as a Graduate Research Assistant at the John Glenn College of Public Affairs and at AEDE.
